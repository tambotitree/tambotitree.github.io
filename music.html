What Music Should Be

Notes from decades of listening, writing, playing, and now building with AI.

Music should begin with a real moment. Not a marketing category, not a genre label, and not a machine filling empty space. A real moment: people eating humba around a table, workers unloading bangus at three in the morning, mosquitoes at the dock when there is no wind and trash has collected water.

AI is useful, but limited. It can help with compression, timing, and shaping a track— even making auto-tune feel more human. But it cannot dance, swat a mosquito, or feel when a line lands. It tends to fill every space with language; after all, it is a language model. It does not (yet) breathe. It does not look at a bar and say, “this part needs space,” or “this will make people move.” It does not know when to hold back, when to lean into the beat, or when to let silence carry the moment. These are human decisions. In this album, AI is used as a tool—never as authorship.

AI can fill space. Music knows when to leave it empty.
Specific moment → shared human experience.

1. Authenticity plus skill

“Three chords and the truth” is not an excuse for laziness. It means the truth has been shaped enough to sing. A real line still needs rhythm. A real story still needs a hook. A true feeling still needs form.

  • Keep the line short enough to remember.
  • Put the strong word where the music lands.
  • Leave space so people can breathe.
  • Let the listener discover the meaning.

2. The more local, the more universal

“Food” is vague. Humba is specific. “Work” is vague. Habwa at alas tres is specific. “Community” is vague. A balikbayan box beside the table is specific.

The local detail is not a limitation. It is the doorway.

3. Music should fit the body

A work song should help the work. A family song should feel like a table. A road song should move forward. If workers can answer the call in a call-and-response chorus, that is not decoration. That is success.

Example: “Alas tres!” → “Habwa na!” works because no one has to think. They can hear it once and join.

4. Do not explain what the song can show

A song about mosquitoes does not need a lecture about sanitation. It needs night, no wind, plastic cups, bottles, standing water, and then the consequence.

The listener understands. Trust them.

5. AI music is useful when it serves the song

Current AI music can imitate genre, but it often misses place, timing, body, and use. The goal is not to press a button and accept whatever comes back. The goal is to use tools to make real songs faster, while keeping control of the human parts: story, hook, rhythm, judgment, and taste.

6. Where we are going

The goal is a practical music pipeline for real life: songs for work, family, training, memory, and community. Some songs will be public. Some will only matter to the people who sing them at the dock.

If the habwa sing the response, victory.
